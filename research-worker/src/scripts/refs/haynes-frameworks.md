# Direct Response Script Frameworks

> Source: Jeremy Haynes — Direct Response Ad Creation Framework SOP
> + Jeremy Haynes Skills (jeremyhaynes.com/skills/)
> All frameworks follow the universal 3-step flow: Hook → Reasons → CTA

---

## Framework 1: Talking Head + B-Roll

**Structure:**
1. Hook: Direct-to-camera, single shot, first 3-5 seconds
2. Body: Cut to B-roll overlays (results screenshots, product demo, environment shots) while voiceover continues
3. CTA: Return to talking head for the close

**When to use:** Default framework. Works for all awareness levels. Best when the speaker has credibility or energy that builds trust on camera.

**Hook patterns:**
- "You Already Know" opener: acknowledge their existing belief, then pivot to your unique angle
- Circumstance opener: reference their specific life situation (role, income, pain)
- Environment match: film in a context that matches their social feed

**Duration:** 30-60 seconds

## Framework 2: Case Study Snapshot

**Structure:**
1. Hook: "How did [Name] go from [Before] to [After] in [Timeframe]?"
2. Snapshot: 15-30 seconds of the client's result (quick bullet, short footage, before/after)
3. CTA: "Watch the same method [Name] used. Click below."

**When to use:** When you have real client results. Best for product-aware and solution-aware prospects who need proof, not education.

**Hook patterns:**
- Name + specific result + timeframe
- Before state → After state contrast
- "Don't believe me? Here's [Name]'s story."

**Duration:** 30-45 seconds. Keep the case study portion to 15 seconds max.

## Framework 3: Objection-First

**Structure:**
1. Hook: Lead with the biggest objection AS the hook
2. Body: Address that single objection in 15-20 seconds with logic + quick story
3. CTA: "Interested? Click now."

**When to use:** When research shows a dominant objection blocking conversion. Each script handles ONE objection only.

**Hook patterns:**
- "You think [objection]? Let me prove you wrong."
- "Everyone says [objection]. Here's what they're missing."
- "The #1 reason people don't [action] is [objection]. But..."

**Duration:** 30-45 seconds. Don't do a 5-minute deep dive. Reduce friction, don't debate.

## Framework 4: Q&A Style

**Structure:**
1. Hook: State or read a real user question
2. Body: Short, direct answer with anecdote or success snippet
3. CTA: "Got more questions? I answer them in [resource]. Click below."

**When to use:** Warm audiences and retargeting. Also works cold if the question is common enough to stop scrollers.

**Hook patterns:**
- "A lot of people ask me: '[question]'"
- "The #1 question I get: '[question]'"
- Read from a screenshot/comment as visual

**Duration:** 30-45 seconds

---

## Framework 5: Demo / Screencast

**Structure:**
1. Hook: "[N] steps I took on my laptop to [result]"
2. Demo: Screen recording or phone screen showing the key steps
3. CTA: "Ready to replicate this? Click to see the detailed version."

**When to use:** Tech products, step-by-step methods, SaaS demos, anything where seeing the process on-screen convinces them it's real.

**Hook patterns:**
- Step count + result promise
- "Watch me do [action] in real time"
- "Here's exactly what my dashboard looks like"

**Duration:** 30-60 seconds

---

## Framework 6: 2-Person Interview

**Structure:**
1. Interviewer lobs a question: "You claim [bold result]. Explain that."
2. You answer quickly, weaving direct response points
3. Interviewer: "So where can folks learn more?" → You give the CTA

**When to use:** Breaks the one-person monologue pattern. Feels more credible if the interviewer is half-skeptical.

**Hook patterns:**
- Start mid-conversation (no intro)
- Interviewer challenges a claim
- "Wait, you're saying [surprising claim]?"

**Duration:** 30-60 seconds max. Micro-bites, not a podcast clip.

---

## Framework 7: Skit / Scenario

**Structure:**
1. Hook: "Meet [character]. They're done with [pain]."
2. Show character fumbling/complaining, then you appear with the solution
3. CTA: "If you feel like [character], watch how we solved it."

**When to use:** Pattern interrupt. Stands out in feeds full of monologues. Best for broad awareness campaigns.

**Hook patterns:**
- Character + relatable frustration
- Quick comedy beat or exaggerated pain
- "POV: You just [frustrating action] for the third time this week"

**Duration:** 30-45 seconds. Tight. Flops if too cheesy or long-winded.

## Haynes Meta-Strategies (apply across all frameworks)

### In-Market Targeting (3-4% of addressable audience)
- Already believe in the category. Don't sell them on "why."
- Sell them on "which" or "who" — your unique approach or system.
- Use "You Already Know" and "You're Doing This, But..." openers.
- Qualify inside the ad: "If you already [do X], this is for you."

### Dynamic Creative Testing
- Supply 4-10 video variations, 3-5 headlines, 3-5 body copy blocks
- Platform auto-serves best combos per user
- Half assets short (30s), half long (60-90s)
- Test hook first (highest impact variable), then format, then angle

### Hammer Them (Post-Opt-In)
- After conversion event, serve 15-20+ short content ads
- Build familiarity, preempt objections, replicate "seen 30 of your videos" effect
- Boosts show-up rates, shortens sales cycles

### Venus Fly Trap Sequence
- 3-video warming sequence: cold → warm → hot
- Video 1: Value/education (no pitch)
- Video 2: Deeper value + light positioning
- Video 3: Direct response CTA

### Creative Refresh Cadence
- Under $2k/month spend: refresh every 60 days
- $2k-$10k/month: refresh every 30 days
- Over $10k/month: refresh every 14-21 days
- Fatigue signals: CTR declining >20% WoW, frequency >3, CPL up >25%

### Hook → Reasons → CTA (Non-Negotiable)
- Hook: First 3-5 seconds. Pattern interrupt. Speak to real-time interests.
- Reasons: Why your approach beats alternatives. 1-2 punchy success points. Stories + data.
- CTA: Direct, clear. Add "so you can" statement. Single action.
