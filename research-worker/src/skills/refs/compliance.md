# Advertising Compliance & Platform Policy Reference

> Source: Google Ads policies, Meta Advertising Standards, LinkedIn Advertising Policies,
> TikTok Advertising Policies (2024–2025). Always verify current policy at platform help centers
> before campaign launch — policies change frequently.

---

## Special Ad Categories (Platform-Specific)

These categories carry the most restrictive targeting limitations. Mis-categorization results in
ad disapproval or account suspension.

### Meta Special Ad Categories

| Category           | Definition                                                  | Targeting Restrictions                                      |
|--------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| Housing            | Real estate sales/rental, mortgage, title insurance         | No age, gender, or ZIP/postal exclusions; limited radius targeting |
| Employment         | Job listings, staffing agencies, recruiting                 | No age, gender exclusions; no geographic exclusions < 50 miles |
| Credit             | Credit cards, loans, auto financing, mortgages              | No age, gender exclusions; limited radius targeting         |
| Social Issues, Elections, Politics | Political/advocacy content | Requires authorization; disclaimers mandatory; no Lookalike |
| Health & Wellness  | Certain health-related products/services                    | No body-related targeting; limited behavioral targeting     |

**Action Required**: When running Housing, Employment, or Credit campaigns on Meta, you MUST select the special ad category in the ad set settings. Failure to do so is a policy violation even if you are not excluding demographics yourself.

### Google Ads Sensitive Category Ads

| Category           | Policy                                                      |
|--------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| Financial products | Must disclose APR/fees; must comply with local laws (FCRA, state laws) |
| Healthcare / Drugs | Prescription drug ads require certification; OTC varies by country |
| Gambling           | Requires country-specific certification; prohibited in many markets |
| Political          | Election ads require identity verification and spend disclosure |
| Employment         | Must comply with non-discrimination laws; no demographic exclusions |
| Housing            | Must comply with fair housing laws; limited targeting options |
| Alcohol            | Permitted in some countries with age-gating; prohibited in others |

## Prohibited Content (All Platforms)

### Universally Prohibited

- **Counterfeit goods**: Fake branded products — immediate account suspension risk
- **Dangerous products**: Weapons, explosives, drugs (illegal), drug paraphernalia
- **Discriminatory content**: Ads that discriminate based on protected characteristics
- **Deceptive claims**: False testimonials, misleading before/after images, unsubstantiated claims
- **Malware / Spyware**: Ads linking to malicious software
- **Adult content**: Explicit sexual content (though some platforms permit regulated adult categories with certification)
- **Tobacco**: Most platforms prohibit tobacco products outright; e-cigarettes restricted/prohibited
- **Illegal products/services**: Hacking tools, stolen data, illegal services

### Google-Specific Prohibitions

- Keyword insertion that implies false urgency ("Limited time — TODAY ONLY" if not actually true)
- Ads that misuse Google brand names (can't say "Advertise on Google" without written permission)
- Clone sites / bridge pages (thin landing pages that redirect elsewhere without value)
- Keyword stuffing in display URLs
- Phone number in headline field (except Call-Only ads)

### Meta-Specific Prohibitions

- Profanity, threatening language
- Before/after weight loss imagery (prohibited regardless of accuracy)
- "Pick one" style imagery implying negative self-perception
- Cryptocurrency ads (restricted — require pre-authorization from Meta)
- Surveillance/tracking tools
- Tabloid-style sensationalism ("You won't believe...")
- Overly personal language implying knowledge of user's situation ("Are YOU diabetic?")

### LinkedIn-Specific Prohibitions

- Generic business opportunity ads without clear product/service
- Get-rich-quick schemes
- Fake testimonials
- Job ads that discriminate by protected class

## Restricted Industries (Require Special Handling)

| Industry           | Platform Issues                                             | Required Actions                                            |
|--------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| Healthcare / Medical | HIPAA compliance for remarketing; sensitive health targeting prohibited on Meta | Exclude medical condition audiences; add FDA-required disclaimers; no before/after |
| Pharmaceuticals    | Google requires LegitScript certification; Meta requires pre-authorization | Obtain certifications before campaign launch; 4–8 weeks lead time |
| Legal Services     | State bar association rules on advertising; no false guarantees | "Results may vary" disclaimers; no "best lawyer" claims    |
| Finance / Lending  | FCRA, TILA, state lending laws; must disclose APR          | APR disclosure in ad copy; credit-check language compliance |
| Alcohol            | Age-gating required; prohibited in certain countries/regions | Activate age gating; geo-exclude prohibited regions        |
| Gambling           | Most platforms require geo-specific certification           | Apply for certification 2–4 weeks before launch             |
| Supplements / Nutraceuticals | FTC guidelines on claims; cannot claim to "cure" or "treat" | "These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA" disclaimer |
| Political / Advocacy | Identity verification required; spend disclosure           | Register as political advertiser; no Lookalike targeting    |
| Dating / Relationship | Restricted on Meta; requires pre-authorization            | Apply for authorization; no sexually suggestive imagery     |
| Firearms           | Cannot advertise to circumvent laws; ammo/accessories have specific rules | No conversion-based advertising in many markets             |

## Disclaimer Requirements by Industry

### Finance

Required disclosures in ad copy (country-dependent):
- APR (Annual Percentage Rate) for lending products
- "Rates vary by credit score" or equivalent
- "See terms at [URL]" for complex financial products
- Risk warnings for investment products: "Capital at risk" / "Past performance is not indicative of future results"

### Healthcare / Medical

- No guaranteed results language ("Cure," "Fix," "Eliminate")
- Before/after images: FDA guidelines prohibit misleading comparisons
- "Individual results may vary" for wellness claims
- "Consult your doctor before..." for medical advice-adjacent content
- HIPAA: Do not use pixel-based retargeting on pages that reveal health conditions

### Legal

- "Results may vary" / "Prior results do not guarantee similar outcomes"
- No use of "best," "top-rated," "number one" without substantiation
- State bar requirements vary significantly — review local rules
- No guarantees on case outcomes

### Supplements / Nutraceuticals

- FDA disclaimer: "These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."
- FTC: Claims must be substantiated by "competent and reliable scientific evidence"
- No disease-claim language (must be structure/function claims only)

### Real Estate

- Fair Housing Act: "Equal Housing Opportunity" logo/statement required in many ad formats
- No discriminatory language (no preferences for/against protected classes)
- Licensing disclosure requirements vary by state

## Landing Page Requirements

All platforms have landing page policies as well as ad policies.

### Universal Landing Page Requirements

| Requirement                    | Detail                                                     |
|--------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| Functional and fast            | Page must load within 3–5 seconds; 404 pages cause disapproval |
| Matches ad content             | Landing page must deliver what the ad promises             |
| Contact information            | Physical address or contact method must be accessible      |
| Privacy policy                 | Required when collecting any user data (forms, pixels)     |
| Terms of service               | Required for subscription or financial products             |
| No interstitials               | Pop-ups that block content before user can view page are prohibited |
| No automatic downloads         | Files cannot download without user action                  |

### Google-Specific Landing Page Rules

- **Destination mismatch**: Display URL domain must match final URL domain
- **Cloaking**: Never show different content to Googlebot vs. users
- **Bridge pages**: Pages whose sole purpose is to redirect to another site are prohibited
- **Minimum content**: Page must have substantive, original content

### Meta-Specific Landing Page Rules

- Landing page must be fully functional (no broken links)
- Must not use "Facebook" or "Meta" in ways implying endorsement
- Must not use unauthorized Facebook/Meta logos or imagery
- Pixel data cannot be used to discriminate in housing/employment/credit

## Trademark Policies

### Google Ads Trademark Use

| Scenario                                         | Policy                                              |
|--------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| Bidding on competitor trademarks as keywords     | Generally **permitted** (bidding only)              |
| Using competitor trademark in ad copy/headlines  | Generally **prohibited** without authorization      |
| Reseller/informational exception                 | Resellers may use trademark if they are authorized  |
| Complaint process                                | Trademark owners can file Google trademark complaint|

### Meta Trademark Use

- Using competitor logos or trademarks in ad creative is prohibited without written authorization
- Comparative advertising allowed but claims must be substantiated
- "As seen in [Media outlet]" requires documentation of authentic coverage

### LinkedIn Trademark Use

- Using company/brand logos in ads requires rights to that content
- Comparative claims must be factually accurate and not misleading

## Account-Level Compliance Checklist

Before launching any new campaign, verify:

- [ ] Ad creative reviewed against platform prohibited/restricted content lists
- [ ] Landing page has privacy policy (especially if pixel/tracking is installed)
- [ ] Special ad category selected on Meta if applicable (Housing/Employment/Credit)
- [ ] Google certification obtained if running pharmaceuticals, gambling, or financial products
- [ ] Disclaimer copy added to ad body where legally required
- [ ] Age targeting set appropriately for age-restricted products
- [ ] Geographic targeting configured to exclude prohibited regions
- [ ] Retargeting audiences exclude sensitive medical/financial condition-based signals
- [ ] All claims in ad copy can be substantiated with evidence on file
