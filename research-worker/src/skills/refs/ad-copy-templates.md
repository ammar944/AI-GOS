# Ad Copy Templates & Formulas

> Source: Copywriting best practices from direct response and digital advertising practitioners
> (2024–2025). Adapt all templates to specific brand voice, audience, and compliance requirements.

---

## Core Copywriting Frameworks

### PAS: Problem → Agitate → Solution

The foundational direct-response formula. Identifies pain, intensifies it, then offers relief.

**Structure:**
1. **Problem**: State the prospect's pain point directly
2. **Agitate**: Amplify why the problem is costly or frustrating
3. **Solution**: Present your product/service as the answer

**B2B Example (SaaS CRM):**
- Problem: "Your sales team is losing leads in spreadsheets."
- Agitate: "Every missed follow-up is a deal your competitor closed instead."
- Solution: "Automate your entire pipeline with [Product]. Free trial — no credit card."

**B2C Example (Fitness App):**
- Problem: "Gym memberships you never use."
- Agitate: "You're paying $60/month for guilt."
- Solution: "10-minute workouts at home. Start free — cancel anytime."

---

### BAB: Before → After → Bridge

Paints a picture of current reality, desired future state, then shows the path between them.

**Structure:**
1. **Before**: Current painful or suboptimal situation
2. **After**: The ideal outcome once the problem is solved
3. **Bridge**: Your product/service is how they get there

**B2B Example (Analytics Platform):**
- Before: "Exporting CSVs at midnight to build Monday's board deck."
- After: "Automated dashboards your team can trust — in real time."
- Bridge: "[Product] connects to your existing tools in 15 minutes."

**B2C Example (Personal Finance App):**
- Before: "Wondering where your paycheck went."
- After: "Watching your savings account grow on autopilot."
- Bridge: "[App] categorizes every transaction and builds your plan for you."

---

### AIDA: Attention → Interest → Desire → Action

Classic funnel model for longer-form ad copy (landing pages, email, display ads with more space).

**Structure:**
1. **Attention**: Interrupt the scroll with a bold headline or visual hook
2. **Interest**: Explain what makes this relevant to the reader
3. **Desire**: Trigger want by highlighting benefits and outcomes
4. **Action**: Give a clear, low-friction call to action

**B2B Example (HR Software):**
- Attention: "What if onboarding took 2 days instead of 2 weeks?"
- Interest: "[Product] automates every checklist, contract, and training module."
- Desire: "3,000 companies cut onboarding time by 60% in their first month."
- Action: "Book a 15-minute demo — see your team's setup live."

## Headline Templates by Ad Type

### Google Ads RSA Headlines (30 characters max)

**Benefit-led headlines:**
- `[Result] in [Timeframe]` — "10x Leads in 30 Days"
- `[Action] Without [Pain Point]` — "Scale Ads Without Guessing"
- `The [Superlative] [Category] Tool` — "The Fastest Invoice App"
- `[Number] [Category] for [Audience]` — "50+ Reports for CFOs"
- `Try [Product] Free — No CC Needed`

**Problem-focused headlines:**
- `Tired of [Pain]?` — "Tired of Missed Follow-Ups?"
- `Stop [Negative Outcome]` — "Stop Losing Hot Leads"
- `[Pain Point] Solved` — "Manual Reporting Solved"

**Social proof headlines:**
- `Trusted by [Number]+ [Audience]`
- `Rated #1 for [Category] — [Year]`
- `[Award/Certification] [Category]`
- `[Notable Client] Uses [Product]`

**Action / urgency headlines:**
- `Get [Outcome] Today`
- `Start Free — [Key Benefit]`
- `[Timeframe] Free Trial Starts Now`
- `Join [Number]+ [Audience]`

**Google Ads RSA Description Templates (90 characters max):**
- `[Product] helps [audience] [achieve outcome] — [proof point]. [CTA] today.`
- `[Key feature 1]. [Key feature 2]. [Key feature 3]. Try free for [timeframe].`
- `[Pain point]? [Product] [solution in one sentence]. No [objection]. [CTA].`

### Meta Ads Primary Text Templates (125 characters optimal)

**Direct problem/solution:**
```
[Pain point]? [Product] [solution]. [Proof or benefit]. [CTA].
```
Example: "Chasing invoices manually? [Product] auto-sends reminders and tracks payments. Get paid 2x faster. Start free."

**Story hook:**
```
"[Relatable situation]." That was [Customer Name/Persona] before [Product].
Now [outcome]. [CTA].
```
Example: '"I spent 3 hours every Monday on reports." That was Sara, a marketing director at a 200-person company. Now her dashboards update themselves. See how.'

**Number-driven proof:**
```
[Company] was [baseline]. After [Product]: [result 1], [result 2], [result 3].
[Timeframe]. [CTA].
```
Example: "Acme had a 40% close rate. After 90 days with [Product]: 65% close rate, 30% shorter sales cycle, 2x pipeline. Join 5,000+ sales teams. Book demo."

**Curiosity / open loop:**
```
Most [audience] don't know [counterintuitive fact]. Here's what [smart audience] do instead.
```
Example: "Most B2B companies waste 60% of their ad spend on the wrong keywords. Here's what fast-growing SaaS companies target instead."

### LinkedIn Ads Introductory Text Templates (150 characters optimal)

**Thought leadership / insight hook:**
```
[Counterintuitive insight or stat]. Here's what top [industry] leaders do differently.
```

**Pain acknowledgment:**
```
If you're still [manual/inefficient process], you're leaving [outcome] on the table.
[Product] changes that.
```

**Social proof for B2B:**
```
[Notable company] cut [metric] by [%] in [timeframe] using [Product].
Here's exactly how they did it.
```

**Direct value proposition:**
```
[Audience role]: [Product] helps you [key outcome 1] and [key outcome 2].
[Proof point]. [CTA] →
```

## Social Proof Patterns

| Pattern                        | Template                                                    | Notes                           |
|--------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------|
| Customer count                 | "Trusted by 10,000+ [audience]"                            | Round numbers feel more credible|
| Named customer logo            | "Used by [Company A], [Company B], [Company C]"            | Permission required; works best for enterprise |
| Specific result + attribution  | "[Company] increased [metric] by [%] in [timeframe]"       | Attribution lifts believability |
| G2 / Capterra rating           | "Rated 4.8/5 on G2 — 500+ reviews"                        | Third-party validation          |
| Industry award                 | "Winner: [Award Name] [Year]"                              | Works well in headline position |
| Media mention                  | "As seen in [Publication A], [Publication B]"              | Brand credibility signal         |
| Net Promoter / CSAT            | "NPS of 72 — top quartile in [category]"                  | Strong for B2B enterprise       |
| Year in market                 | "[X] years helping [audience] [outcome]"                   | Trust signal; longevity         |

## Urgency Formulas

Use genuine urgency only — manufactured urgency damages trust.

**Time-based (genuine):**
- "Offer ends [specific date]"
- "[X] days left — [offer]"
- "Available through end of quarter"
- "Early access closing [date]"

**Scarcity-based (genuine):**
- "Only [X] spots remaining in [cohort/program]"
- "Beta program — limited to 50 companies"
- "[X] licenses left at founding member pricing"

**Risk-reduction / low-friction urgency:**
- "Start in 5 minutes — no contract"
- "Cancel anytime — first [X] days free"
- "No credit card required to start"
- "30-day money-back guarantee"
- "Setup in one afternoon"

**Competitive urgency (factual):**
- "Your competitors are already using [category]"
- "See what you're missing vs. the market"

## CTA Best Practices

### CTA Hierarchy by Funnel Stage

| Stage                | CTA Examples                                               | Friction Level |
|----------------------|------------------------------------------------------------|----------------|
| Top-funnel (awareness)| "Learn More," "See How It Works," "Watch Video"           | Zero friction  |
| Mid-funnel (consideration)| "Get Free Guide," "See a Demo," "Compare Plans"     | Low friction   |
| Bottom-funnel (intent)| "Start Free Trial," "Book a Demo," "Get a Quote"          | Medium friction|
| Conversion           | "Buy Now," "Subscribe," "Get Started," "Claim Offer"      | Higher friction|

### Power Words for CTAs

| Category     | Words                                                              |
|--------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Speed        | Instantly, Now, Today, In minutes, Fast                            |
| Ease         | Simple, Easy, Effortless, In one click, Without hassle             |
| Value        | Free, Save, Exclusive, Bonus, Limited                             |
| Ownership    | Get, Claim, Grab, Unlock, Access                                  |
| Safety       | Guaranteed, No risk, Cancel anytime, No credit card, Money-back   |

## B2B vs. B2C Copy Tone Guide

| Dimension        | B2B                                              | B2C                                               |
|------------------|--------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| Primary driver   | ROI, efficiency, risk reduction                  | Emotion, aspiration, identity                     |
| Decision maker   | Multiple stakeholders (committee)                | Individual or household                           |
| Proof type       | Data, case studies, ROI calculators, logos       | Testimonials, UGC, before/after, reviews          |
| Tone             | Professional, authoritative, peer-to-peer        | Conversational, aspirational, fun                 |
| Length           | Longer explanations accepted                     | Shorter; hook fast                                |
| Objection focus  | "Will this integrate?" "What's the contract?"   | "Is it worth the price?" "Does it really work?"  |
| Value language   | "Save 10 hours/week" "Increase revenue by 20%"  | "Finally feel confident" "Look your best"         |

## Ad Copy Testing Framework

### What to Test First

1. **Headline** — largest impact on CTR (test 3 radically different value props)
2. **Primary emotion** — pain vs. gain messaging
3. **Proof type** — statistics vs. testimonials vs. named customer
4. **CTA** — high-friction ("Book Demo") vs. low-friction ("Learn More")
5. **Format** — static image vs. video vs. carousel

### Test Cell Structure (Meta)

- Minimum: 2 creative variants per ad set; identical targeting
- Recommended: 2–4 variants; run until statistical significance or 1,000 impressions per variant minimum
- Kill threshold: CTR < 50% of control; CPL > 130% of control after 500+ impressions
- Declare winner: CTR difference > 20% sustained over 3+ days with meaningful impression volume

### Test Cell Structure (Google RSA)

- Allow Google to serve all variants; review asset performance report after 4+ weeks
- "Best" and "Good" assets: keep; "Low" assets: rotate out after 30 days
- Pin only when legally necessary — pinning limits RSA learning
