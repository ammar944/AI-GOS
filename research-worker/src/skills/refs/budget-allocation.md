# Budget Allocation Frameworks for Paid Media

> Source: Industry benchmark aggregates (2024–2025). Frameworks derived from practitioner consensus
> (Tier 1 media buyers, agency playbooks, platform best practice guides).

---

## Core Framework: 70/20/10 Rule

The foundational budget allocation model for mature paid media programs.

| Bucket       | Allocation | Purpose                            | Criteria for Inclusion                                   |
|--------------|------------|-------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| **Proven**   | 70%        | Known winners — defend and scale   | 3+ months of data; CPA ≤ target; consistent volume       |
| **Testing**  | 20%        | Validated experiments               | 4–8 week tests with defined success criteria             |
| **Experimental** | 10%   | Emerging channels/formats/audiences | Hypothesis-driven; acceptable to lose entire budget      |

### Applying the 70/20/10

- **Proven bucket**: Scale proven campaigns incrementally (10–20% budget increase per week maximum; see ramp-up schedule below).
- **Testing bucket**: Each test should have a hypothesis, success metric, and kill date. Minimum test budget = enough to reach statistical significance (typically 50–100 conversions per variant).
- **Experimental bucket**: One experiment at a time unless team has bandwidth to monitor. New channels, new creative concepts, new audience strategies.

### When to Shift Allocations

| Scenario                                | Shift Recommendation                                      |
|-----------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------|
| No proven campaigns yet (new account)   | 50% testing, 40% proven candidates, 10% experimental     |
| Proven campaign hits frequency cap      | Move 10–15% from proven to testing                        |
| Strong test result (CPA ≤ target)       | Graduate to proven; replenish testing with new tests       |
| Market disruption / seasonality         | Temporarily shift 10% from experimental to proven defense |
| Scaling phase (aggressive growth)       | 80% proven, 15% testing, 5% experimental                  |

## Minimum Viable Budget by Platform

These are monthly minimums to exit the learning phase and get statistically meaningful data.

| Platform            | Absolute Minimum | Recommended Minimum | Notes                                           |
|---------------------|------------------|---------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| Google Search       | $1,500/mo        | $3,000 – $5,000/mo  | Below minimum = too few conversions for Smart Bidding |
| Google Shopping     | $1,000/mo        | $2,500 – $5,000/mo  | Product feed quality matters more than budget   |
| Meta Ads            | $1,000/mo        | $3,000 – $5,000/mo  | Advantage+ needs ≥ $50/day to learn             |
| LinkedIn Ads        | $1,500/mo        | $4,000 – $8,000/mo  | $50/day minimum per campaign required by platform|
| TikTok Ads          | $500/mo          | $2,000 – $4,000/mo  | Creative refresh cost must be factored in       |
| YouTube Ads         | $1,000/mo        | $2,500 – $5,000/mo  | CPM model; need reach to justify               |
| Google Performance Max | $2,000/mo     | $5,000 – $10,000/mo | Needs diverse conversion signals to optimize    |

> **Rule of thumb**: Monthly budget should be at least 10x your target CPA to allow the algorithm
> to gather enough conversion data during the learning phase (Google recommends 30–50 conversions
> per campaign per month for Smart Bidding to stabilize).

## Budget Ramp-Up Schedule

### New Account / New Campaign

| Week       | Budget Level | Focus                                             |
|------------|--------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| Week 1–2   | 25% of target | Baseline setup; confirm tracking; observe CPCs   |
| Week 3–4   | 50% of target | Pause non-performers; identify early winners      |
| Week 5–8   | 75% of target | Shift budget to winners; begin A/B creative tests |
| Week 9–12  | 100% of target| Full budget deployed; Smart Bidding stabilized    |
| Week 13+   | Scale as needed| +10–20% budget increase per week if CPA holds   |

### Existing Account Scaling

- **Safe increment**: Increase budget by no more than 20% per week to avoid disrupting algorithm learning.
- **Reset threshold**: Increasing a campaign budget by >50% in one change may trigger a new learning phase.
- **Scaling signal**: CPA stable for 2+ consecutive weeks at current budget = ready to scale.
- **Diminishing returns signal**: CPAs rising while volume stays flat = at market saturation for current targeting.

---

## Scaling Triggers

When to increase budget on a campaign:

1. **CPA ≤ 90% of target** for 2+ consecutive weeks
2. **Impression share lost to budget** > 20% (Google Search)
3. **Auction insights** show competitors entering your key terms
4. **Seasonal opportunity** approaching (Black Friday, fiscal year-end, etc.)
5. **Creative freshness** confirmed — new winning ad identified
6. **ROAS ≥ 110% of target** (e-commerce)

## Saturation Signals (When to Stop Scaling)

Stop increasing budget when you observe:

| Signal                                | What It Means                                              |
|---------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| Frequency ≥ 3.5 on Meta (7-day)       | Audience saturated; creative or audience refresh needed    |
| Impression share > 90% (Google)       | Maxed out on available searches; bid higher or expand      |
| CPAs rising > 20% week-over-week      | Hitting diminishing returns; pause and diagnose            |
| CTR declining > 30% over 4 weeks      | Creative fatigue; rotate in fresh ads                      |
| Reach Curve flattening (Meta)         | New audiences required                                     |
| Marginal ROAS < 1.0                   | Each incremental dollar is losing money                    |

### Frequency Caps by Platform

| Platform        | Recommended Max Frequency | Notes                                           |
|-----------------|---------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| Meta (Cold)     | 2.0 – 3.0 / week          | Above 3.5 = creative fatigue signal             |
| Meta (Retarget) | 5.0 – 8.0 / week          | Warm audiences tolerate higher frequency        |
| LinkedIn        | 3.0 – 4.0 / week          | Professional context; burnout faster than Meta  |
| YouTube         | 3 – 5 impressions / week  | Skippable formats tolerate higher frequency     |
| Display/GDN     | 5 – 10 / week             | Banner blindness is real; test higher floors    |

## Seasonality Patterns

### Universal Seasonality Modifiers (CPM/CPC Premium vs. Annual Average)

| Period                   | CPM/CPC Change | Action                                            |
|--------------------------|----------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| Q4 (Oct–Dec)             | +20% – +50%    | Increase budget 25–40%; protect ROAS with floor bids|
| Black Friday / Cyber Monday| +40% – +80%  | Max budget 7 days; pre-load creative by Nov 1     |
| January–February         | –10% – –20%   | Opportunity to grow share; lower CPMs             |
| Summer (June–August, B2B)| –10% – –15%   | Reduce B2B budgets; maintain B2C                  |
| Tax Season (Feb–Apr, Finance)| +20% – +40%| Finance/tax verticals only                        |
| Back-to-School (Jul–Sep) | +20% – +35%   | Education, apparel, e-commerce                    |

### Industry-Specific Seasonality

| Vertical       | Peak Months          | Trough Months        | Budget Recommendation                          |
|----------------|----------------------|----------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| E-commerce     | Oct – Dec            | Jan – Feb            | 2x–3x peak budget vs. trough                   |
| SaaS           | Jan – Mar, Sep – Nov | Jun – Aug, Dec       | Maintain during trough; scale at fiscal periods|
| Real Estate    | Mar – Jun            | Dec – Jan            | Follow listing volume in local market          |
| Legal          | Jan – Mar, Sep – Oct | Jun – Aug            | Aligns with post-holiday and fiscal cycles     |
| Healthcare     | Jan (new insurance)  | Summer               | Maximize Q1 budget for insurance-driven demand |
| Travel         | Apr – Jun (summer)   | Nov – Jan            | Counter-seasonal opportunity in Jan            |
| Education      | Jul – Sep (enrollment)| Jan – Mar           | Course registrations peak pre-semester         |

## Budget-to-Attribution Window Alignment

| Attribution Model    | Implication for Budget                                       |
|----------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------|
| 7-day click / 1-day view (Meta) | Standard; align budget cycles to weekly rhythm    |
| 30-day click (Google) | Conservative; allows longer-cycle products to attribute    |
| Data-driven (Google) | Requires ≥ 300 conversions/month in account to activate    |
| Last-click           | Undervalues awareness spend; Meta/YouTube appear inefficient|
| First-click          | Overvalues discovery campaigns; inflate top-funnel budgets  |

> **Recommendation**: Use data-driven attribution when volume qualifies. Otherwise use 7-day click
> / 1-day view on Meta and 30-day click on Google. Never compare platforms using different windows.
