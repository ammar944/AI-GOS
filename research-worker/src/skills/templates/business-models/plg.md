# PLG (Product-Led Growth) Business-Model Template

> Source: Wes Bush PLG playbook + David Skok SaaS metrics. Applies to freemium, free-trial, and self-serve SaaS. Layers ON TOP of the industry template (e.g. saas.md) — does not replace it.

**Signals this model applies:** free trial, freemium, self-serve signup, low-to-mid ACV ($5–$500/mo), time-to-value measured in minutes to days.

---

## Funnel

Visit → Signup → Activation → Paid Conversion → Expansion

**DO NOT USE:** lead → MQL → SQL → opportunity → customer. That funnel is SLG.

**Stage definitions:**
- Signup: account created (email verified)
- Activation: user completed the core "aha moment" (product-defined — e.g. sent first message, published first post, ran first report)
- Paid Conversion: moved from free/trial to paid plan
- Expansion: upgraded tier, added seats, or exceeded usage-based threshold

---

## KPI Framework (directional — NO client-specific targets)

| KPI | What it measures | Drivers | Industry range (benchmark) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cost per signup | Paid efficiency at top | Creative + targeting + landing page | $2–$40 depending on category |
| Activation rate | Product onboarding quality | Product UX, onboarding flow | 20–60% |
| Time-to-value | Speed from signup to activation | Product friction | Minutes to hours for good PLG |
| Free-to-paid rate | Monetization quality | Upgrade triggers, pricing, value gap | 2–10% |
| Expansion rate | Retention + value realization | Product maturity, customer success | 5–20% net |

**Never publish client-specific CAC, CPL, or LTV:CAC targets.** PLG unit economics depend entirely on activation rate + retention — variables paid media does not control. If the plan needs a number, publish industry benchmark ranges labeled "(industry benchmark)".

## Default Channel Mix

| Channel | Share | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Meta (FB/IG) | 50–70% | Primary acquisition | Best for PLG awareness + signup flows |
| YouTube | 15–25% | Awareness + educational | Product demo clips, tutorials |
| TikTok | 10–20% | Awareness (B2C / B2SMB) | Only if buyer is younger / non-enterprise |
| Google Search | 0–20% | Bottom-funnel capture | Only if awareness level is solution-aware+ |

**NOT IN MIX by default:**
- LinkedIn (wrong buyer for PLG — individual user or team lead, not enterprise committee)
- Display / programmatic (too passive for PLG signup intent)
- Direct mail / OOH (no PLG fit)

**Budget tier rules:**
- ≤£2k/mo: Meta only
- £2k–£5k: Meta + one supplement (YouTube or TikTok)
- £5k–£15k: Full 3-platform mix + small Google search
- £15k+: Full mix + LinkedIn only if expanding into enterprise tier

## Campaign Types

**Allowed:**
- Signup conversion (primary)
- Video views / reach (for unaware markets)
- Retargeting (ONLY if pixel has accumulated 1k+ events; do not propose at launch)

**Forbidden / avoid:**
- Separate "trial conversion" campaign — the trial IS the conversion event, there's no intermediate stage
- Lead-gen form campaigns (no lead stage in PLG funnel)
- Demo-booking campaigns (no demo in self-serve PLG)
- Middle-of-funnel campaigns at launch (no audience to target yet)

---

## Creative Approach

- **Product demo clips** — 15–30 second walkthroughs showing core value in action
- **Social proof** — user counts, company logos, "X companies switched from Y"
- **Problem-agitation** (if awareness level is unaware/problem-aware) — lead with the pain the prospect hasn't yet named
- **Speed/friction framing** — "set up in 90 seconds", "no credit card required", "free forever"

---

## Gotchas

- Don't force a lead-to-SQL rate — there is no SQL stage
- Don't require a demo booking — contradicts the self-serve model
- Don't publish CAC targets — payback depends on activation + retention (outside paid media)
- Don't forecast customer counts from CPL × conversion rate — PLG conversion windows are long (often 14–30 days post-signup), so short-window attribution is misleading
- Sales cycle is bounded by trial length (see sales-cycle-bounding methodology)
- Growth of signups ≠ growth of revenue — ensure the plan distinguishes between the two

---

## When PLG is hybrid with SLG

Some PLG products have a sales-assisted tier (e.g. self-serve up to 10 seats, sales-team above). In that case:
- Plan the self-serve signup funnel as PLG
- If the client wants enterprise tier expansion, add a second mini-plan using SLG template (usually LinkedIn-led) — but only if budget >£5k AND research supports enterprise TAM

Do not blend the two funnels into one plan.
