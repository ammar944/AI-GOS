# E-commerce Business-Model Template

> Applies to DTC brands, Shopify stores, online retailers with direct-purchase transaction model. Layers on top of industry template (ecommerce.md) with business-model-specific funnel and KPI guidance.

**Signals this model applies:** online store, checkout on-site, physical or digital goods, AOV $20–$500, purchase decision made in-session or within 7 days.

---

## Funnel

Impression → Click → Session → Add-to-Cart → Checkout → Purchase → Repeat

**Stage definitions:**
- Session: landed on site, browsed
- Add-to-Cart (ATC): product added but not purchased
- Checkout: payment details entered but not completed
- Purchase: first order complete
- Repeat: second+ order

---

## KPI Framework (directional — NO client-specific targets)

| KPI | What it measures | Drivers | Industry range (benchmark) |
|---|---|---|---|
| CPC (cost per click) | Paid efficiency | Creative + targeting | $0.50–$3 depending on category |
| CTR | Creative quality | Hook + visual | 1–4% on social |
| ATC rate | Product-market fit + price | Site UX, product page, price | 5–15% of sessions |
| Conversion rate | Checkout quality | Site performance, trust signals | 1–5% of sessions for DTC |
| AOV (average order value) | Pricing + bundling strategy | Upsell/cross-sell, bundles | Brand-specific |
| ROAS | Marketing efficiency | Creative + offer + catalog | 2–5x for mature brands, 1.5–3x for launch |
| MER (marketing efficiency ratio) | Holistic efficiency | Total ad spend / total revenue | 3–8x healthy |
| Repeat purchase rate | LTV / retention | Product quality, email, post-purchase | 15–40% |

**Never publish client-specific ROAS targets.** ROAS depends on margin, catalog, creative, offer — variables the media plan partially influences but cannot guarantee. Publish industry benchmark ranges.

## Default Channel Mix

| Channel | Share | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Meta (FB/IG) | 40–60% | Primary acquisition + retargeting | Advantage+ Shopping for prospecting |
| Google (Shopping + Search) | 20–35% | Bottom-funnel + category capture | Shopping for catalog, Search for branded + category |
| TikTok | 10–25% | Discovery + awareness | Essential for apparel, beauty, consumer goods |
| Pinterest | 5–15% | Visual discovery | For home goods, fashion, wedding, decor categories |
| YouTube / Influencer | 5–15% | Awareness + authority | UGC + reviews |

**NOT IN MIX by default:**
- LinkedIn (wrong buyer unless premium B2B ecommerce)
- Display / programmatic at scale (low ROAS)

**Budget tier rules:**
- ≤£2k/mo: Meta only, catalog-based Advantage+ Shopping
- £2k–£10k: Meta + Google Shopping
- £10k–£25k: Full 3-platform (Meta + Google + TikTok)
- £25k+: Add Pinterest + influencer + diversification

## Campaign Types

**Allowed:**
- Meta Advantage+ Shopping (prospecting + retargeting)
- Catalog-based Dynamic Product Ads (DPAs)
- Google Performance Max
- Google Shopping (standard)
- Abandoned cart retargeting
- Post-purchase retargeting (cross-sell, upsell)
- Influencer / UGC campaigns

**Forbidden / avoid:**
- Lead-gen forms (no lead stage in e-commerce)
- Demo-booking (no demo)
- Signup campaigns (no free tier)

---

## Creative Approach

- **Product-first** — product in frame within first 1 second
- **UGC / authenticity** — creator content, unboxings, real customer reviews
- **Before/after** — beauty, fitness, home, apparel
- **Social proof** — review counts, ratings overlays
- **Offer-driven** — discount code, free shipping threshold, bundle deal
- **Fast hooks** — 3-second attention capture for social feed

---

## Gotchas

- ROAS is the headline KPI but don't publish client-specific targets — depends on margin + AOV + creative
- Catalog health determines ceiling — weak product feed = weak campaigns regardless of media plan
- Seasonality matters — plans must address Q4 / BFCM / category-specific peaks
- Iframe pixel / server-side tracking essential — iOS 14.5 broke pure client-side attribution
- AOV boost strategies (bundles, thresholds) often have higher ROI than ad spend increases
- First-order CAC vs LTV divergence — don't confuse first-order ROAS with customer LTV

---

## Sales cycle

E-commerce cycles are short:
- Impulse categories (fashion, beauty, snacks): same-session to 24 hours
- Considered purchases (electronics, home goods): 1–7 days
- Luxury / high-ticket: 7–30 days

Attribution windows must match cycle — 7-day click / 1-day view for most DTC; 28-day only for luxury.
