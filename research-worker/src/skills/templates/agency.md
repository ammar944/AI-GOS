## Creative Strategy

### Content-Led Marketing for Agencies

Agencies must demonstrate expertise before earning a prospect's trust. Creative strategy should reflect this through content that showcases knowledge.

**Content Funnel:**

| Stage         | Content Type                                    | Ad Format                    | CTA                                   |
|---------------|-------------------------------------------------|------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| Awareness     | Trend insight / market prediction / data study  | LinkedIn doc or video        | "Follow for more" or "Read full report" |
| Education     | Framework, methodology, checklist, guide        | Lead gen form (PDF download) | "Get the free guide"                  |
| Consideration | Case study: client problem → approach → result  | LinkedIn carousel, video     | "See how we did it"                   |
| Evaluation    | Webinar, live AMA, "office hours"              | Lead gen form                | "Reserve your spot"                   |
| Decision      | Free audit, strategy session, scorecard        | Contact form, calendar link  | "Get your free [audit/strategy call]" |

### Thought Leadership Creative Guidelines

- **Founder/practitioner voice**: Content from named individuals outperforms brand-only content by 5–10x on LinkedIn
- **Specificity over generality**: "We increased a SaaS company's CAC by 40% in Q1" > "We drive results"
- **Framework sharing**: Proprietary frameworks make content unique and shareable
- **Data/research**: Original surveys or data analysis generates 3–5x more engagement than opinion alone
- **Controversy/counterintuitive**: "Why [accepted best practice] is wrong" — high engagement but requires substantiation

### Case Study Ad Formula

Structure: Client industry + problem → approach (not full detail) → measurable result → CTA

Example:
- "A Series A SaaS company was burning $40K/mo on ads with 0 MQLs. We rebuilt their targeting in 30 days. Result: 4x MQL volume at the same budget. See the case study →"

### Free Offer (Lead Magnet) Types

| Offer Type               | Value Level | Conversion Rate (landing) | Best For                           |
|--------------------------|-------------|---------------------------|------------------------------------|
| Free audit (personalized)| Very High   | 5% – 15%                 | High ACV clients; bottom funnel    |
| Strategy call (30 min)   | High        | 3% – 10%                 | Consultative selling; custom work  |
| PDF guide / checklist    | Medium      | 15% – 35%                | Top-funnel; list building          |
| Webinar / workshop       | High        | 5% – 15%                 | Mid-funnel education               |
| Template / calculator    | Medium-High | 10% – 25%                | Task-specific tool for ICP pain    |
| Report / benchmark data  | High        | 10% – 20%                | Data-driven buyers; B2B            |

## Targeting Guidelines

### LinkedIn ICP Targeting for Agencies

**Define ICP by:**
- Company size: Align to your sweet spot (e.g., 50–500 employees for most agencies)
- Industry: Industries where you have strong case studies (start there)
- Seniority: Director+ for most B2B services; Manager+ if they own the budget
- Job function: Marketing, Sales, Operations, IT (depends on what you sell)

**High-performing title targeting for agencies:**
- Marketing Directors / VPs / CMOs (if selling marketing services)
- CEOs / Founders / Owners (for SMB with single decision-maker)
- CTO / IT Director (for technology consulting)
- COO / Head of Operations (for operations consulting)
- CFO / Finance Director (for financial advisory/accounting)

**Title targeting pitfall**: LinkedIn job titles are self-reported and inconsistent. Test both exact titles and job function + seniority combinations.

### Google Search for Agency Services

**High-intent keywords:**
- `[service type] agency [city]` / `[service type] agency for [industry]`
- `hire [service specialist]`
- `[service type] consulting firm`
- `outsource [service type]`
- `[result] agency` (e.g., "lead generation agency")

**Problem-intent keywords:**
- `how to get more [result] for [business type]`
- `[problem] solution for [company size/industry]`

**Avoid/negate:**
- `freelance` (unless you want freelancers as clients or are positioning as fractional)
- `jobs`, `salary`, `career`
- `what is [service]` (informational; low buyer intent)
- `free` (unless your offer is genuinely free and you want to attract that)

## Budget Guidelines

### Minimum Monthly Budget by Agency Size

| Agency Size / Stage     | Minimum Monthly Budget | Notes                                           |
|-------------------------|------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| Solo / micro (<$500K)   | $1,500 – $3,000        | LinkedIn thought leadership; Google brand       |
| Small agency ($500K–$2M)| $3,000 – $8,000        | LinkedIn prospecting + Google Search            |
| Mid-size ($2M–$10M)     | $8,000 – $25,000       | Full mix; ABM for dream accounts                |
| Large ($10M+)           | $25,000+               | Multi-channel; content investment heavy         |

**Budget allocation philosophy for agencies:**
- 60% content-driven (LinkedIn thought leadership, YouTube, case study amplification)
- 30% direct response (lead gen forms, Google Search for in-market)
- 10% retargeting (website visitors, video viewers, lead form openers)

### Organic + Paid Integration

Agencies typically rely on a blended strategy:
- **LinkedIn organic** (founder posting): Free but time-intensive; should run always
- **Paid amplification**: Boost best-performing organic posts at $500–$2,000/mo as Sponsored Content
- **New audience reach**: Paid campaigns beyond followers; core spending area

### Attribution for Agencies

Agency new business often has 3–6 month consideration cycles. Attribution challenges:
- **First-touch attribution**: Captures what created awareness (LinkedIn post, Google ad)
- **Last-touch**: Captures final trigger (Google brand search, referral)
- **Practical approach**: Ask every inquiry "How did you hear about us?" — simple CRM tracking
- **LinkedIn benchmark**: Expect LinkedIn leads to close 2–3x slower than Google intent leads
- **Account-based reporting**: Track engaged target accounts over time, not just individual leads
