Option A: Sequential Artifact Studio

After onboarding, the hero CTA becomes Start research. The screen transitions into a cinematic studio where exactly one artifact is “on stage” at a time. A left rail shows the section queue. The center is the live artifact canvas. The right rail is the section’s sub-agent thread with Looks good and Request changes.

  • Best fit for one-by-one artifact arrival and visible generation.
  • Most premium-feeling because the center canvas gets full visual focus.
  • Sub-agent chat stays contextual instead of becoming another generic inbox.
  • Lets you use advanced frontend touches: staged reveals, citation chips, diff glow, live proof logs.

Option B: Orchestration Board + Artifact Drawer

The primary surface is a board of section cards showing state, progress, and summaries. Clicking a card opens the artifact in a right-side drawer or modal with its own sub-agent chat.

  • Strong if you want users to see the whole research program at once.
  • Feels operational and agentic, but less magical than a staged artifact canvas.
  • Risks making each artifact feel secondary because the board becomes the main surface.

Option C: Dual-Pane Live Build Surface

The center view splits into a live generation pane and the resulting artifact pane. The sub-agent chat docks beneath or to the right. This is the most “advanced AI UI” feeling, but it can easily look busy during onboarding-era research.

  • Best if your product’s identity is “watch the machine build”.
  • Great for motion and streaming spectacle, but harder to read and approve.
  • Higher implementation and interaction complexity than the value likely justifies here.
