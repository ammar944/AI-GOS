2. INDUSTRY_ANALYSIS_SKILL

Target runner: research-worker/src/runners/industry.ts

A. TAM/SAM/SOM Calculation Methodology

Sources: McKinsey Market Sizing Primer, CB Insights VC methodology, bottom-up SaaS benchmarks

Skill Knowledge MARKET SIZING DERIVATION RULES: - Always attempt bottom-up sizing first: Count (potential buyers matching ICP) x (ACV for this product tier) State derivation: "~30K US agencies with 5-50 employees x $15K/yr = $450M SAM" - Ratio benchmarks when bottom-up data unavailable: - B2B SaaS (US-focused): SAM ~ 8-15% of published category TAM - MarTech/AdTech: SAM ~ 15-25% of category TAM (high online distribution) - Consumer apps: SAM ~ 20-40% of TAM (geography/platform bounded) - E-commerce tools: SAM ~ 12-20% of category TAM - SOM estimate: 1-5% of SAM (seed/early), 3-10% (growth), 5-15% (scale-up) - Growth rate anchors by maturity: - Early-stage markets: 40-100% YoY TAM growth - Growth-stage markets: 15-40% YoY - Mature markets: 3-15% YoY (GDP + category premium) - Declining markets: <3% or negative - Never cite a parent-market TAM as direct niche size - Always state the derivation trace in output

B. Market Lifecycle Stage Diagnostics

Sources: Rogers Adoption Curve, Gartner Market Guide taxonomy

Skill Knowledge MARKET MATURITY DIAGNOSTIC SIGNALS: Maps to enum: early | growing | saturated EARLY: - Buyers still defining the problem, not searching category keywords - G2/Capterra: no dedicated category page OR <20 reviews - <5 funded competitors in the space - Category name not yet standardized across vendors - Paid media: Problem-aware targeting required, category education ads - CPC environment: Low (untargeted market), but CVR also low GROWING: - Category keywords being actively bought by 3+ competitors - G2/Capterra: dedicated category with 20-200 reviews - 5-20 funded competitors, category leader emerging - VC funding actively flowing (new rounds in last 12 months) - CAGR typically 20-60% in industry reports - Paid media: Solution comparison, category ownership, speed to market - CPC environment: Rising 20-40% YoY as competition heats up SATURATED: - 1-3 dominant players with >40% market share - G2/Capterra: 200+ reviews, quarterly top-lists published - Analyst reports exist (Gartner MQ, Forrester Wave, G2 Grid) - M&A activity: leaders acquiring smaller players - Price compression visible (free tiers, aggressive trials) - Paid media: Differentiation on niche/price/service, high-intent capture only - CPC environment: Peak competitive, high-intent keywords expensive

B. JTBD Decomposition for Ad Targeting

Sources: Ulwick ODI, Moesta/Spiek demand-side JTBD, HubSpot 2025 State of Marketing

Skill Knowledge JTBD DECOMPOSITION — for each ICP persona output: 1. Functional job: What task are they literally trying to accomplish? → Maps to feature/benefit ads (what it does) — landing page content 2. Emotional job: How do they want to FEEL after completing it? → Maps to aspirational creative (how you'll feel) — ad hooks 3. Social job: How do they want to be PERCEIVED by peers/boss? → Maps to social proof / prestige creative — highest B2B conversion 4. Pain consequence: What happens if the job is NOT done? → Urgency driver for cold traffic hooks 5. Workarounds: How are they doing this job today without the solution? → "Still doing [workaround]?" hooks outperform "Introducing [product]" B2B ads that lead with emotional/social job in creative (hooks attention) and satisfy functional job on landing page (converts intent) outperform feature-first ads by 2.3x CTR (HubSpot 2025). BUYING TRIGGER TAXONOMY: - Push trigger: Painful event forces action (system broke, audit failed) → Best for urgency hooks - Pull trigger: Aspirational event (competitor raised money, new VP hired) → Best for LinkedIn - Anxiety trigger: Fear of falling behind / FOMO → Best for retargeting and warm audiences - Habit trigger: Contract renewal, new fiscal year, new hire onboarding → Best for seasonal campaign timing

4. OFFER_ANALYSIS_SKILL

Target runner: research-worker/src/runners/offer.ts

A. Hormozi Value Equation

Sources: Alex Hormozi "$100M Offers", direct response copywriting canon

Skill Knowledge HORMOZI VALUE EQUATION: Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice) Apply to every offer dimension: - Dream Outcome: Does the headline name the specific end-state? Specificity multiplies perceived value. "$47K revenue increase" > "improve revenue" - Perceived Likelihood: What proof mechanisms increase believability? Numbered case studies beat vague testimonials. Money-back guarantees increase perceived likelihood 30-50%. - Time Delay: Speed to first value = primary cold traffic conversion lever. Free trials, instant access, "done-for-you" reduce this. "Results in 7 days" > "Results over time" - Effort & Sacrifice: "We do it for you" outperforms "here's how to do it" for cold traffic by 2-5x CVR. Every paid media offer can be scored on these four levers. Hook = Dream Outcome. Body copy = Perceived Likelihood. CTA = Time Delay reduction. Offer framing = Effort reduction.

B. Value Ladder / Offer Stack Assessment

Sources: Russell Brunson "DotCom Secrets", direct response offer architecture

Skill Knowledge OFFER STACK ASSESSMENT (Cold Traffic Viability): Check whether an entry-point offer exists before the core product: - Lead Magnet / Free Value ($0): Risk-free micro-commitment. CPL ads only. CVR: 5-15% from cold paid traffic (top quartile: 20-30%) - Tripwire / SLO ($7-$97): Designed to cover ad spend from first purchase. Best for DTC and info products. Single-session conversion viable. - Core Offer ($97-$10K): Requires 2-4 touch nurture for cold traffic if over $200/mo. Demo-led for higher price points. - Profit Maximizer (upsell/cross-sell): 40-80% of revenue per customer. Never advertised directly to cold traffic. COLD TRAFFIC VIABILITY CHECK: 1. Is there a risk reversal (guarantee, free trial, free tier)? 2. Is there a fast first win the buyer gets immediately? 3. Are bonuses/inclusions named and valued explicitly? 4. Is the offer named (not just described)? RED FLAG: No entry-point + high-ticket core offer = flag as "cold traffic viability concern — no low-friction entry point"

C. SaaS Pricing Cold Traffic Conversion Thresholds

Sources: WordStream, Unbounce, ConversionXL aggregated conversion data 2024-2025

Skill Knowledge SAAS PRICING x COLD TRAFFIC CONVERSION THRESHOLDS: Under $50/mo: Direct purchase viable. Single-session conversion. CVR 3-8% from cold paid traffic. Free trial with credit card works. $50-$200/mo: Free trial or demo required. 2-4 touch nurture. CVR 1-3% for demo request from cold traffic. $200-$500/mo: Demo-led sales. 14-30 day cycle. Paid ads drive MQL, not direct purchase. CVR 0.5-1.5%. $500+/mo: Account-based approach. Ads support brand recall + retargeting. CVR 0.3-0.8% from cold traffic. Requires multi-touch attribution. Below 0.3% expected CVR = DO NOT LAUNCH signal. Flag in redFlags when pricing suggests this threshold. CONVERSION RATE BENCHMARKS BY OFFER TYPE (2025): - SaaS free trial from cold traffic: 2-5% (top quartile: 8-12%) - B2B demo/lead form: 1-3% (top quartile: 5-7%) - E-commerce direct purchase: 1-4% (top quartile: 6-10%) - Lead magnet / content download: 5-15% (top quartile: 20-30%) - Webinar registration: 15-35% (top quartile: 40%+) - High-ticket application: 2-8% (top quartile: 12-15%) CHANNEL-SPECIFIC LANDING PAGE CVR (2025): - Google Search: 4-8% avg for lead gen (highest intent) - Meta (FB/IG): 1-3% lead gen, 0.5-2% direct purchase - LinkedIn: 0.5-1.5% CVR, highest quality leads, highest CPL ($50-300+) - YouTube: 1-2% from pre-roll for consideration offers - TikTok: 1-4% DTC under $100, declining for higher AOV

D. Price Anchoring Techniques

Sources: Direct response copywriting, SaaS pricing psychology research

Skill Knowledge PRICE ANCHORING TECHNIQUES (ranked by cold traffic effectiveness): 1. Hiring cost anchor (B2B, highest converting): "Replaces a $75K/yr hire" — makes software pricing feel trivially small 2. Agency cost anchor: "Agencies charge $5K/mo for this. Our tool does it for $197/mo" 3. Time cost anchor: "Saves 10 hours/week. At $100/hr, that's $4,000/mo recovered" 4. Named competitor anchor: "[Competitor] charges $300/user/mo. We're $49 flat" 5. ROI anchor: "4.2x ROI in 90 days" — requires proof, highest trust ceiling 6. Per-unit anchor: "Just $1.63/day" — works for sub-$60/mo products 7. Risk reversal anchor: "14-day free trial, no credit card" — highest trial CVR VALUE GAP FORMULA for messaging: "You pay $X/mo. You get [specific outcome worth $Y/mo] back." Explicit value gap quantification converts skeptical B2B buyers. OFFER DIFFERENTIATION SCORING (ranked by cold traffic conversion): 1. Speed: "Results in 7 days" — highest urgency, highest conversion 2. Specificity: "$47K increase per client" — 2-5x higher CTR than vague 3. Risk elimination: "We do it for you / full refund" — removes #1 objection 4. Social proof density: Logos, review counts, case study specifics 5. Exclusivity: "Only for [specific ICP qualifier]" — premium perception 6. Mechanism specificity: "Our proprietary [named process]" — blocks comparison RED FLAGS FOR PAID ADS (Offer Dimension): - "Contact sales for pricing" with no price anchor = cold traffic bounce - Pricing page requires email = low conversion from paid traffic - No risk reversal (guarantee/trial/refund) = high objection rate - Vague outcomes ("improve your business") = low relevance score - Time-to-value >30 days = unsuitable without nurture sequence - No named differentiator = competes on price only, margin erosion
