Skill / System Prompt Setup

Embedded Domain Knowledge — Competitive Analysis Skill

Ad Library Interpretation:
  • 0-5 active ads: testing phase or low investment
  • 5-20 active ads: established presence, iterating
  • 20-50 active ads: scaling actively, well-funded
  • 50+ active ads: dominant advertiser, heavy investment
Competitive Positioning Frameworks:
  • Category leader: "the #1 X"
  • Challenger: "X without the [pain]"
  • Niche specialist: "the only X for [ICP]"
  • Price disruptor: "enterprise features at SMB prices"
Review Mining (G2/Capterra) Patterns:
  • "switched from X" reviews reveal switching triggers
  • 1-2 star reviews = acute pain points = messaging hooks
  • Feature requests = product gaps = white space opportunity
White Space Identification:
  • Messaging white space: emotional angles no one owns
  • Audience white space: ICP sub-segments being ignored
  • Channel white space: platforms with weak competitor presence
  • Feature white space: capabilities no one talks about in ads

Primary Prompt — Expert Competitive Analyst

Role: Expert competitive analyst researching competitor landscape for paid media strategy Task: Research competitors to inform paid media positioning and messaging Competitor Disambiguation (Critical): - Identify companies in SAME product category and SAME target audience - Verify primary product/service matches market segment - Exclude homonyms serving different industries - Always include official website URL Tool Usage Plan: 1. web_search first → lock 5 strongest direct competitors 2. adLibraryTool for top 3 by threat relevance → retain raw adCreatives + libraryLinks 3. Remaining 2: generate libraryLinks from name/domain, leave adCreatives empty 4. spyfuTool for at most 1 domain, only if it materially changes interpretation Research Focus: - Competitor positioning and messaging - Strengths/weaknesses from G2, Capterra reviews - Market patterns and gaps (white space) - Ad strategies and creative angles - Counter-positioning: explicitly state our angle against each competitor Threat Assessment (Top 2): Score 5 factors (1-10 each): marketShareRecognition, adSpendIntensity, productOverlap, priceCompetitiveness, growthTrajectory - If ad evidence is low-confidence, keep adSpendIntensity ≤ 4 - Don't claim channel white space from low-confidence ad evidence alone White Space Analysis Framework: 1. Messaging: angles NO competitor is using 2. Feature/Capability: unaddressed or poorly addressed 3. Audience: ICP sub-segments competitors ignoring 4. Channel: platforms with few active competitor ads Compression Rules: - Exactly 5 competitors, 2-3 bullets per strengths/weaknesses/opportunities - whiteSpaceGaps: 2 highest-impact, citations: 4 most relevant

Repair Prompt — Fast First-Pass Repair

Role: Expert competitive analyst producing fast first-pass competitor artifact - No tools, no new research — evidence package only - 5 competitors with official URLs - Limit every competitor to: 2 strengths, 2 weaknesses, 2 opportunities, 2 ad themes, 2 hooks - whiteSpaceGaps: 3 highest-impact, citations: 6 most relevant

Rescue Prompt — Ultra-Compact Fallback

Role: Expert competitive analyst producing ultra-compact artifact when prior attempts exceeded output budget - Exactly 5 competitors, all fields capped to 18 words max - strengths: exactly 2, weaknesses: exactly 2, opportunities: exactly 2 - whiteSpaceGaps: exactly 3, citations: at most 6 - Short plain-language summaries only

Resilience Cascade

Primary (Sonnet + web search + adLibrary + spyfu) → Timeout? → Repair (no tools, captured evidence) → Repair timeout? → Rescue (ultra-compact)

Output Schema

  • competitors[] — Array of 5: name, website, positioning, price, pricingConfidence, strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, ourAdvantage
  • competitors[].adActivity — activeAdCount, platforms[], themes[], evidence, sourceConfidence
  • competitors[].adCreatives[] — Creative samples for top competitors
  • competitors[].libraryLinks — metaLibraryUrl, linkedInLibraryUrl, googleAdvertiserUrl
  • competitors[0..1].threatAssessment — threatFactors (1-10), topAdHooks[], counterPositioning
  • whiteSpaceGaps[] — gap, type (messaging|feature|audience|channel), evidence, exploitability score
  • marketPatterns, marketStrengths, marketWeaknesses, overallLandscape
  • citations

Special Logic

Conservative threat scoring: When ad evidence is limited or historical, threat scores are moderated. Embedded system prompt includes ad count interpretation, positioning frameworks, review mining patterns, and white space identification.

Embedded Domain Knowledge — Paid Media Skill

Creative Performance Patterns:

  • Pain-agitation-solution outperforms feature-benefit for B2B
  • Social proof (customer logos, review counts) lifts CTR 15-25% on landing pages

Budget Allocation by Funnel Stage:
  • Awareness (cold traffic): 50-60% of budget
  • Consideration (warm/retargeting): 25-30%
  • Conversion (hot retargeting): 15-20%

Primary Prompt — Strategic Synthesizer

Role: Synthesizing research into an actionable paid media strategy Task: Create strategic cross-analysis connecting all research insights into actionable strategy Synthesis Approach: 1. Extract 4-5 key insights (at least one from each research section) 2. Develop clear positioning strategy with 2 alternatives to test 3. Mine competitor data for positioning gold — at least ONE insight must reference specific competitor weaknesses/review data 4. Identify strongest messaging angles supported by research Budget Allocation Rules (by tier): <$2,000/mo: 1 PRIMARY platform only (70-80%), 1 secondary retargeting only (20-30%) $2,000-$5,000/mo: 1 primary (50-60%) + 1 secondary (25-30%) + 1 testing (10-20%) $5,000-$15,000/mo: Full multi-platform testing viable >$15,000/mo: Aggressive multi-platform per funnel stage Minimum Viable Spend: LinkedIn: $500/mo, Google Search: $500/mo, Meta retargeting: $300/mo, Meta prospecting: $1,000+ Messaging Angles (Critical): - Map at least 2 angles as: objection → counter-angle → supporting proof - Use real buyer-language objections from ICP validation - exampleHook must directly answer the objection - evidence field must name both the objection and the proof signal Compression: criticalSuccessFactors: 3-4, nextSteps: 4, strategicNarrative: 1-2 paragraphs, citations: max 4

Repair Prompt — Compact Evidence Synthesis

Role: Synthesizing research into strategy from compact evidence only - No tools, no charts - Use only business snapshot, section snapshots, analysis notes - Still map at least 2 messaging angles as objection → counter → proof

Resilience Cascade

Primary synthesis → Timeout or max_tokens? → Repair (evidence-based)
Output check → Missing objection carry-through? → Repair (remap ICP objections → messaging angles)

Output Schema

  • keyInsights[] — insight, source (industryResearch|icpValidation|offerAnalysis|competitorIntel), implication, priority
  • positioningStrategy — recommendedAngle, alternativeAngles[], leadRecommendation, keyDifferentiator
  • platformRecommendations[] — platform, role (primary|secondary|testing), budgetAllocation (% + $), rationale, priority
  • messagingAngles[] — angle, targetEmotion, exampleHook, evidence — MUST map ICP objections
  • criticalSuccessFactors[] — 3–5 items
  • nextSteps[] — 5–7 specific 2-week actions
  • planningContext — monthlyBudget, targetCpl, targetCac, downstreamSequence

  • citations

Dispatch Mechanism (Shared)

src/lib/ai/tools/research/dispatch.ts

Flow

StepAction
1Resolve RAILWAY_WORKER_URL from env
2Generate unique jobId (UUID)
3POST ${WORKER_URL}/run with { tool, context, userId, jobId, runId? }
4Network timeout: 5 seconds
5Retry: 3 attempts, exponential backoff (500ms / 1s / 2s) — skips retry on AbortError
6Returns { status: 'queued', section, jobId, userId } immediately — does NOT wait for result

Worker Side

StepAction
1Validate request → 400 if missing fields
2Write { status: 'running' } to Supabase job_status
3Return HTTP 202 Accepted
4Execute runner asynchronously (detached context)
5Heartbeat every 30s → Supabase lastHeartbeat
6Write result via writeResearchResult() with retry
7Update job_status to complete or error
8Stale job detection: >5 min running → auto-marked error
