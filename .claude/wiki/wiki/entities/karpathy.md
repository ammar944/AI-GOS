# Andrej Karpathy

Former head of AI at Tesla; founding member of OpenAI; educator (Stanford CS231n); now independent — primary sources in this wiki. Operating mode right now: vibecoding with agents, auto-research, open-source education.

## Why he shows up in this wiki

Two primary sources cite him directly:
- The LLM-wiki pattern itself (originated in his X post and Herk's follow-up explainer).
- The No Priors conversation with Noam on coding agents, token throughput, jaggedness, physical-world lag.

## Positions worth remembering

- **"Something flipped in December."** Stopped hand-coding. Shifted to agent delegation.
- **Not inside a frontier lab on purpose.** Values independence over being-in-the-room. Would consider a tour basis.
- **Ensembles of people > any single lab.** Wants more labs, not fewer. Same reason ML always uses ensembles.
- **Pro open source.** Wants a Linux-equivalent for AI — not necessarily frontier-capable, but an industry-wide working space.
- **Physical-world lag is real.** Digital first, robotics later.
- **"The things agents can't do is your job now."** Be strategic about where you're spending attention.

## Projects referenced

- **nanoGPT / makemore / microGPT** — the boil-it-down progression. microGPT is now ~200 lines.
- **Dobby the claw** — home automation agent he built for himself. WhatsApp interface.
- **No Priors podcast** — with Noam from Conviction; the source transcript here.
- **LLM wiki** — the knowledge-base pattern documented in this vault.

## Recurring Karpathy tropes

- "Skill issue" — when agents fail, blame the setup not the model.
- "Jaggedness" — PhD + 10-year-old at the same time.
- "Cognitive core" — the reasoning substrate that should be shared across specialized models.
- "AI psychosis" — the feeling of what one person can suddenly do with agents.
