# Jaggedness

Karpathy's term for the lived paradox of using current LLMs: "an extremely brilliant PhD student who's been a systems programmer for their entire life, and a 10-year-old." Simultaneously. Shift by task.

## The cause

RL. Labs improve models on anything verifiable — code, math, scientific reasoning, agentic task completion. Soft things — jokes, nuance, clarifying questions, emotional calibration — don't have a reward signal, so they don't improve. "You're either on rails and you're part of the superintelligence circuits, or you're not on rails and everything meanders."

## Karpathy's proof

ChatGPT's joke. Three years ago: "Why don't scientists trust atoms? Because they make everything up." Today: same joke. Meanwhile, the same model moves mountains in agentic-task benchmarks.

## Implications for how you use an agent

- **Put it on rails where it shines.** Defined tasks, verifiable outputs, code, math, structured extraction.
- **Do the soft work yourself.** Framing, prioritization, taste, customer empathy. The model will give you a plausible version of these and it will be the boring wrong version.
- **Stop being surprised when it bombs a soft question.** That's not a bug. That's what jaggedness looks like from the outside.

## For AIGOS specifically

- Runners (industry, ICP, competitors, offer, keywords, media-plan): on rails. Structured Zod output, verifiable against source.
- Script humanize pass: partially on rails (the 43-point audit is verifiable-ish, but taste isn't).
- "Should we change the product direction?": off rails. Do not outsource this.

## Companion concept
