# Research Sections (canonical spec)

Source of truth for the 6 positioning Sections that compose an Audit. Each Section has a fixed set of named **sub-sections** below — these drive the typed shape of each Section's Artifact schema. Do not invent sub-sections that are not listed here; do not collapse this structure into a generic envelope.

---

**01. Market & Category Intelligence** — What market is this, and what's happening inside it?

* Category definition and the adjacent categories buyers confuse it with
* Market size and trajectory signals (public data, funding flows, hiring velocity, search trend direction)
* Structural forces moving the market (regulation, platform shifts, buyer-behavior shifts)
* Category maturity (emerging / growing / consolidating / commoditizing) with evidence

**02. Buyer & ICP Validation** — Does the ICP they described exist in the wild, and in what shape?

* ICP existence check — account counts by firmographic cut
* Persona reality — titles, seniority, team size, org-chart position
* Awareness-level distribution across the ICP (unaware → most-aware, with evidence per level)
* Buying context — observable triggers that move accounts from passive to active
* Where they actually cluster — communities, newsletters, events, podcasts, Slack groups

**03. Competitor Landscape & Positioning** — Who are they really competing with, and how is each of those competitors framing the market?

* Full competitor set — direct, indirect, status-quo, DIY
* Positioning taxonomy — how each competitor describes the problem and their solution
* Pricing reality — public prices, gated-pricing signals, packaging patterns
* Share-of-voice map — who owns which search terms, communities, publications
* Public strengths/weaknesses — from reviews, verbatim customer complaints, analyst mentions
* Competitor narrative arc — villain, hero, transformation claim

**04. Voice of Customer & Objection Evidence** — What do real buyers say — in their own words, not our guesses?

* Pain language — verbatim quotes from reviews, forums, support threads, sales-call transcripts
* Objection evidence — what actually stops purchase, phrased how buyers phrase it
* Switching stories — what made buyers leave a prior solution
* Stated decision criteria — what buyers say matters in evaluation
* Success-state language — how buyers describe the "after"

**05. Demand & Intent Signals** — Where does active demand live, and what is it asking?

* Keyword demand — volume, intent type, current-ranking reality
* Question mining — People Also Ask, Reddit, Quora, community threads
* Content-gap evidence — topics with demand and weak competitor answers
* Intent signals available in the wild — relevant job postings, RFPs, news triggers
* Event and community signal map — where conversations are actually happening

**06. Offer & Performance Diagnostic** — What does the company's own evidence say about offer-market fit and where the funnel breaks?

* Offer-market fit evidence — proof points from their own reported metrics
* Funnel diagnosis — where conversion actually breaks, against reported CAC / LTV / cycle / MRR
* Channel truth — what has and hasn't worked, with quantified evidence, not opinions
* Retention and activation health — do customers stay and reach the first value moment
* Red flags in their own numbers — contradictions between claimed motion and actual math
