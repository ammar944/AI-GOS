01

The gist

AI-GOS takes a company's website URL and turns it into a full strategic marketing blueprint — six expert reports on the market, the buyer, the competition, what customers actually say, demand, and how strong the offer is.

The big move we locked: the fast, reliable section engine we perfected in a separate "lab" project becomes the real engine inside AI-GOS — replacing the old one that was slow and kept stalling. It's now wired and deployed to a preview. The work ahead: prove it on real data, harden the backend to our course's standard, and swap the model to DeepSeek to cut cost ~10×.

02

What the app does

It's form-driven, not a chatbot — no AI asks you questions. You hand it a URL; research fills in the rest.

  1. 1

    Paste a website URL

    The one input that starts everything.

  2. 2

    Prefill research runs — URL → onboarding

    It reads the actual website (scrapes the real pages) and searches the web, then extracts verified facts to fill the onboarding fields and write the descriptions — product, ICP, value prop, competitors, and the rest. Every value carries a source; anything it can't verify is left blank, never guessed. /api/onboarding/research · scrape + web research

  3. 3

    Review & edit the brief

    You confirm or correct the auto-filled fields. the "GTM Brief"

  4. 4

    Submit → deep research builds the "corpus"

    A heavier pass that gathers evidence and facts into one pile — the raw material every section reads. deepResearchProgram = corpus + onboarding fields

  5. 5

    Confirm the brief once more

    Now the corpus is in — a last check before the heavy work starts.

  6. 6

    Six sections fan out in parallel

    The engine writes all six positioning reports at once (a few at a time).

  7. 7

    Cards render live → saved to profile

    Each report appears as soon as it finishes.

The six reports it produces:

positioningMarketCategory

Market & Category

What market you're really competing in.

positioningBuyerICP

Buyer / ICP

Who buys, and what they care about.

positioningCompetitorLandscape

Competitor Landscape

Who you're up against and how they position.

positioningVoiceOfCustomer

Voice of Customer

What real customers say — in their words.

positioningDemandIntent

Demand & Intent

What buyers are searching for and asking.

positioningOfferDiagnostic

Offer Diagnostic

How strong the offer is, and where it breaks.

03

The decision we locked

There are two codebases. This is about which one's engine wins.

AI-GOS is home. It owns the parts that work well: onboarding, the deep research that builds the corpus, the database, login, and hosting. None of that changes.

The "lab" (ai-gos-ai-sdk-lab) is where we built and hardened a better section-writing engine — the answer-tool engine.

The old thinking was that the lab is just a sandbox to copy ideas back from. The locked decision flips that: the lab's engine is the backend. We wired it straight into AI-GOS and rip out the old section execution — the slow, unreliable path that took roughly 540 seconds (about 9 minutes) per section and sometimes just froze. The new path reads the corpus we already built, so it's fast and there's no waiting on live web crawling.

Old — being replaced

06

Proper backend, grounded in the course

What "no app hanging off a thread" means here — the AIHero AI SDK v6 course's discipline, not a new queue engine. These principles govern P2.

the anti-hang rule

Bounded lifecycle

Every run is bounded: watchdog < route maxDuration. Client-disconnect & server-abort are terminal states, not background mysteries. "Done" = output validated + minimums pass + persisted.

no single thread

Decoupled execution

Fan out per-section so no one serverless invocation owns the whole 6-section run. Each section independently bounded and retryable.

ToolLoopAgent

Explicit stopWhen

A reusable, typed agent with explicit stop conditions — never the default loop length.

Output.object

v6 structured output

streamText + Output.object + partialOutputStream; Zod + minimums before persist. Never layer Output.object on the answer tool (the #411 trap).

data-* events

Typed events

Structured progress / validation / final events. v1 maps these to Supabase events; per-token streaming stays deferred.

ArtifactEnvelope

Persistence boundary

Persist the artifact only after it validates; serialize same-run writes (compare-and-swap on revision).

Langfuse / OTel

Observability, not optional

Trace live runs — prompt, model, usage, status, failure — plus AI SDK DevTools locally.

deterministic first

Evals as gates

Schema + minimum checks gate before persistence; datasets for the historically flaky sections (Voice of Customer, Competitor Landscape).
