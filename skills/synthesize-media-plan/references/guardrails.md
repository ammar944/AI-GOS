# Guardrails - synthesize-media-plan

## What To Generate

Generate a sourced media plan that a downstream script writer and workspace card can consume directly. Keep the output concise and structured.

## What Not To Generate

- Finished ad scripts.
- Production naming conventions.
- Visual asset specifications.
- Numeric forecasts for leads, acquisition cost, or revenue.
- Unsupported benchmark ranges.
- Placeholder values.
- Supabase persistence payloads.

## Block Discipline

### Strategic Frame

Use the locked brief and upstream positioning to determine business model, awareness level, sales-cycle ceiling, and in-market tier mix.

### Channel Mix

Recommend one to three channels only. Each channel must cite why the ICP, demand evidence, competitor landscape, positioning, or brief constraints justify it.

### Audience-Campaign Matrix

Use one campaign for small budgets and no more than two campaigns per phase. The campaign objective and audience must both be sourced derived claims.

### Creative Angle System

Create three to eight angle directions. Keep them as strategic creative angles, not finished copy.

### Sales Process Guidance

Only include process advice that connects paid traffic to conversion path, sales handoff, activation, or proof needs.

### Benchmarks

Use zero to two benchmarks. Each benchmark must be interpreted and tied to exactly two process-side levers.

### Rollout Phases

Use two to four phases. Every phase needs objectives, campaigns, and a decision gate.

### Strategy Snapshot

Summarize only what the plan already supports. Do not introduce a new platform, objective, or benchmark in the snapshot.
