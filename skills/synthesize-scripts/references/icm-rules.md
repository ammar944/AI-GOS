# ICM Script Rules

ICM means every script is built from intent, claim, and message:

1. Intent: match the awareness level and in-market tier.
2. Claim: use only sourced evidence from the locked brief or upstream skill outputs.
3. Message: produce hook, middle, and CTA lines that preserve line-level provenance.

## Awareness Handling

- `unaware`: start from the buyer's lived problem; avoid product-first copy.
- `problem`: name the pain and buying trigger before introducing a solution.
- `solution`: compare methods and sharpen the differentiator.
- `product`: handle risk, proof, and switching objections.
- `mostAware`: keep the CTA direct and reason-specific.

## Pack Shape

- A full pack contains 3 scripts per selected awareness tier.
- A valid pack has 9 to 15 scripts.
- Each tier must rotate `meta`, `google`, and `linkedin`.
- Each tier must rotate `video`, `static`, and `email`.
- No tier may repeat the same primary angle.

## Provenance

Every hook, middle line, CTA, hook variant, and objection line must include:

- `derived_from`: at least one upstream skill or `gtm-brief`
- `evidence`: at least one claim with `source_url` and `retrieved_at`

Do not turn a competitor hook into finished copy. Competitor evidence may only be used for counter-positioning.
